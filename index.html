About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Robotics at KAIST (Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology), affiliated with the Vehicular Intelligence Lab under Prof. Dongsoo Har. My thesis — "Explainable AI for Safer Roads Using Multimodal Framework" — investigates how vision-language foundation models can make autonomous perception systems both accurate and interpretable.

My work spans self-supervised visual-language learning, cross-modal knowledge distillation, concept bottleneck models, and edge-AI optimization. I have published in IEEE Transactions on ITS (IF 8.5), ISPRS (IF 12.2), and other top venues, with two papers featured in KAIST Breakthrough.

Before KAIST, I spent several years as a research consultant on smart-city AI projects in India, collaborating with Deloitte India and securing grants from the University of Edinburgh and the Government of India. My Indian Patent (No. 538566) is deployed in Hyderabad and Bangalore for adaptive traffic signaling.

Key Research

Featured Projects

IEEE T-ITS · Under Review
VisText-Net: Explainable Accident-Prone Feature Discovery
Dual-branch CLIP-based concept bottleneck model combining CNN and CLIP text encoder. Automated concept discovery via mechanistic interpretability of linear perceptron weights with counterfactual validation and optimal transport (Sinkhorn normalization) for patch-to-concept assignment.
64.25% confidence drop (counterfactual) 39–63% reduction (CAM ablation) CLIP + CBM
IEEE T-ITS 2025 · IF 8.4
TIME-VAD: Text-Informed Vehicle Accident Detection
Reversed CLIP magnitude assumption for accident detection. Magnitude-Enhanced Multiple Instance Learning with text-anchor contrastive loss and Dilated Temporal Conv-Attention (DTCA) block (pyramid rates 1,2,4). Cross-domain generalization demonstrated CCD→DAD.
94.44% ROC-AUC (DoTA) +9.74% over SOTA 99.97% AP (CCD) KAIST Breakthrough
IEEE T-ETCI · Under Review
Zero-Shot Road Anomaly Detection
Triple-CLS architecture with controlled noise injection for cross-modal synchronization. Zero-shot captioning without paired training data. Systematic foundation model evaluation: CLIP variants, BLIP, LLaVA — analysing accuracy-latency-size trade-offs for edge deployment.
92.42% accuracy +16–22% over VLMs 15–25× smaller Zero-Shot
ISPRS 2026 · IF 12.2
Cross-Modal Wildfire Detection (Edge AI)
Thermal-to-optical knowledge transfer: CLIP teacher (frozen) → CNN student (trainable). Spatial feature distillation via Pearson correlation; semantic-relation distillation via cosine similarity affinity matrices. Optimized for aerial edge deployment.
100% image-level accuracy 2.99 GFLOPS 0.004s inference Edge-AI
