About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Robotics at KAIST (Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology), affiliated with the Vehicular Intelligence Lab under Prof. Dongsoo Har. My thesis — "Explainable AI for Safer Roads Using Multimodal Framework" — investigates how vision-language foundation models can make autonomous perception systems both accurate and interpretable.

My work spans self-supervised visual-language learning, cross-modal knowledge distillation, concept bottleneck models, and edge-AI optimization. I have published in IEEE Transactions on ITS (IF 8.5), ISPRS (IF 12.2), and other top venues, with two papers featured in KAIST Breakthrough.

Before KAIST, I spent several years as a research consultant on smart-city AI projects in India, collaborating with Deloitte India and securing grants from the University of Edinburgh and the Government of India. My Indian Patent (No. 538566) is deployed in Hyderabad and Bangalore for adaptive traffic signaling.

Key Research

Featured Projects

IEEE T-ITS · Under Review
VisText-Net: Explainable Accident-Prone Feature Discovery
Dual-branch CLIP-based concept bottleneck model combining CNN and CLIP text encoder. Automated concept discovery via mechanistic interpretability with counterfactual validation and optimal transport (Sinkhorn) for patch-to-concept assignment.
64.25% confidence drop39–63% CAM ablationCLIP + CBM
IEEE T-ITS 2025 · IF 8.4
TIME-VAD: Text-Informed Vehicle Accident Detection
Reversed CLIP magnitude assumption for accident detection. Magnitude-Enhanced MIL with text-anchor contrastive loss and Dilated Temporal Conv-Attention (DTCA) block. Strong cross-domain generalization (CCD to DAD).
94.44% ROC-AUC (DoTA)+9.74% over SOTA99.97% AP (CCD)
IEEE T-ETCI · Under Review
Zero-Shot Road Anomaly Detection
Triple-CLS architecture with controlled noise injection for cross-modal synchronization. Zero-shot captioning without paired training. Systematic benchmarking of CLIP, BLIP, LLaVA for edge deployment trade-offs.
92.42% accuracy+16–22% over VLMs15–25x smaller model
ISPRS 2026 · IF 12.2
Cross-Modal Wildfire Detection (Edge AI)
Thermal-to-optical knowledge transfer: frozen CLIP teacher to trainable CNN student. Spatial distillation via Pearson correlation, semantic distillation via cosine affinity. Optimized for aerial edge deployment.
100% image-level accuracy2.99 GFLOPS0.004s inference
